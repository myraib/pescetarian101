What is a pescetarian?

Overview:

A pescetarian is someone who does not eat any types of meat except for fish and seafood. There are many reasons why people become a pescetarian. A pescetarian diet is made up of mostly plant based, whole grains, nuts, legumes, produce and healthy fats and fish and seafood as a protein source. Many types of pescetarian diets include dairy and eggs, or animal products.

What do pescetarians eat:

  • Whole grains and grain products

  • Beans, lentils, and tofu

  • Nuts,peanuts and seeds

  • Seeds, including hemp,chia and flax seeds

  • All dairy products

  • Fruits

  • Vegetables

  • Seafood

  • Eggs

What pescetarians can not eat:

  • Beef

  • chicken

  • Pork

  • Lamb

  • Turkey

  • Poultry

  • Any types of other meat excluding fish and seafood

Shrimp Pasta Recipie

Ingredients for the crispy breadcrumbs:

  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter

  • 1/2 cup fresh or Panko breadcrumbs

  • 1/8 teaspoon kosher salt

  • 1/8 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

  • Pinch cayenne pepper

  • Pinch garlic powder

    • Ingredients for the shrimp pasta:

  • Pinch garlic powder

  • Cooking spray

  • 1/2 cup whole-milk plain Greek yogurt

  • 2 tablespoons Asian chili-garlic sauce, such as sambal oelek

  • 1 teaspoon honey

  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder

  • Juice of 2 medium limes (about 1/4 cup), divided

  • 12 ounces dry spaghettir

  • 1 pound uncooked peeled and deveined medium shrimp

  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, plus more for the pasta water

  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

  • 1/8 teaspoon cayenne pepper

  • 2 medium scallions, thinly sliced, divided

    • Cooking the shrimp pasta:

    1. Arrange a rack in the middle of the oven and heat to 400Â°F. Lightly coat a rimmed baking sheet with cooking spray; set aside.

    2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Meanwhile, whisk the yogurt, chili-garlic sauce, honey, garlic powder, and half of the lime juice together in a small bowl; set aside.

    3. When the water is boiling, add the pasta and cook until al dente, about 10 minutes or according to package instructions. Meanwhile, pat the shrimp dry and place on the prepared baking sheet. Season with the salt, black pepper, and cayenne and stir to coat. Spread into an even layer. Roast until the shrimp are opaque and pink, stirring once halfway through, 6 to 8 minutes total. Drizzle the remaining lime juice over the shrimp and toss to coat, scraping up any flavorful bits on the baking sheet.

    4. Drain the pasta and return it to the pot. Pour in the yogurt sauce and toss to evenly coat the pasta. Add the shrimp and any juices from the baking sheet, along with half of the scallions, and gently toss again. Generously sprinkle each serving with the crispy breadcrumbs and remaining scallions. Serve immediately.
